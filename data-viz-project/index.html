ICE Detention in Arizona

A data-driven look at immigration detention facilities across the state

By Raphael Romero Ruiz | December 3, 2025

As of June 23, Arizona's six immigration detention centers housed an average of 4,089 individuals daily, according to data from the Transactional Records Access Clearinghouse, a nonprofit, nonpartisan organization that collects, researches and distributes data from federal agencies like ICE.

Source: Syracuse University's Transactional Records Access Clearinghouse (TRAC), ICE facility data as of June 23, 2025

The death of the 75-year-old Cuban national detained at a Florida facility at the end of June brings the total number of people who have died in ICE custody to 13 so far in fiscal year 2025, surpassing the total number of deaths from the previous year in just half the time.

Source: Datawrapper

Congress allocated nearly $3.5 billion to fund ICE's capacity to hold immigrants in custody, resulting in 41,500 beds in detention centers. The passage of the budget reconciliation bill, otherwise known as the "One Big Beautiful Bill," in early July increases spending for immigration detention to $45 billion. That money will be used to increase bed capacity to more than 100,000, according to estimates from groups like the American Immigration Lawyers Association.

Source: Datawrapper

Most of the people held at Eloy do not have criminal records, according to ICE's facilities data, as is the case with several of Elle Delgado's clients.
